Quaggy Morris Constitution

1. Aims and Objectives

  1. To maintain the tradition of morris dancing and music
  2. To represent morris dancing as open, inclusive and culturally relevant
  3. To offer a challenging and healthy activity for members of the side
  4. To support the community by attending local events
  5. To foster connections with local traditions ancient and modern
  6. To enjoy the dancing, socialising and camaraderie that comes with a welcoming, diverse and hospitable team
  7. To encourage the standard of dancing and music amongst the side's members
  8. To maintain an apolitical organisation

2. Membership

  1. Membership is open to all adults who have:
    1. a reasonable level of fitness
    2. commitment to practice to dance with energy
    3. the ability to dance in time to the music
  2. Inexperienced dancers will be invited to join the side in the Autumn to allow for practice
  3. Experienced dancers maybe admitted at any time at the discretion of the Foreman
  4. After attending at least one practice session the Foreman will decide whether to offer individuals a trial of 3 practice sessions
  5. At the end of a trial, the side will vote to decide on whether to invite the individual to join the side
  6. Individuals will not be required to pay subs until invited to join the side
  7. All prospective members will be made aware of the rules
  8. Side members include dancers and musicians

3. Officers

  1. Leader/squire/guardian
    1. They set the general style and approach of the side. This could be reflected by the events attended, the look of the side in public, the types of dances, a focus for a new season or more.
  2. All other jobs and roles to be defined and named by a majority of members to carry out the required jobs to support the side
  3. In the case that more than one member wants to do a job, the meeting will decide in an anonymous vote and by a simple majority
  4. Rules of office:
    1. All officers must be agreed at an AGM or EGM
    2. Officers are in post for 1 year
    3. All officers must present a report to the AGM summarising their work for the year
    4. At least two signatories are required for the bank account. All transactions require two signatories for approval. These signatories will be agreed and recognised at each AGM
    5. In the case of an officer resigning, as EGM will be held to elect a replacement officer

4. Marketing and Publicity

  1. How to attract new members:
    1. A range of local dance outs
    2. Making a range of marketing materials such as regular social media posts and cards/leaflets/badges to distribute
    3. Open practice sessions to allow people to try out
    4. Incorporating joining in dances when out to identify possible new dancers
    5. Maintaining a good level of fun and enjoyment of morris
  2. A website will be maintained to offer information regarding
    1. how to book the side
    2. how to join the side
    3. rules of side membership
    4. style of morris dancing
    5. background and history of the side
    6. upcoming events
    7. material relevant to members such as audio, video and written description of dances and music
  3. All marketing and publicity will be delivered and maintained by specific officers
  4. All content to reflect the side's aims and objectives

5. Bookings and events

  1. Dance outs will be suggested by the side, and if agreed will be organised by the Secretary
  2. The Secretary will find out about the event before it has been booked including:
    1. which other sides will attend, if any
    2. the venue, including directions, parking, access by public transport, etc.
    3. the nature of the event including the start and end times and duration of dancing
    4. the requirements of the side including refreshments, toilets and appropriate conditions for dancing (e.g. noise, location of dancing, etc.)
    5. payment if applicable
  3. Establish local dance outs and invite other sides to join us
  4. Establish a diary that can be published in advance
  5. Attend local dance outs in order to establish and maintain good relations with local sides and community groups

6. Kit and costume

  1. The Foreman will decide when a member goes into costume
  2. New members in costume will wear the 'L' plate for their first season dancing out if they are comfortable doing so
  3. The dancing costume is detailed in the Kit list and consists of:
    1. Black fitted jean breeches to knee length (not jeggings)
    2. Plain white cotton unbranded collarless shirts with white buttons extending the full length, tucked in with sleeves rolled up to the elbow
    3. Black belt (optional)
    4. Knee length socks, one blue on the right leg and one green on the left, colours matching the rest of the team
    5. Blue and green ribbons colour-matched to the team attached at the elbow
    6. Bell pads on each leg as defined in the photo
    7. Black trainers
    8. White hankies that fall to the floor when held by the side
  4. The musicians' costume will align to the style of the dancers including:
    1. A plain white collarless shirt tucked in (as above)
    2. A waistcoat in the colours of the side (matching blue and green) without logos
    3. Black trousers or breeches
    4. Black trainers or shoes
  5. All changes to the costume can be suggested by any member of the side but will only be adopted with the agreement of the Costumier before voted in by a majority
  6. All kit purchased by the side remains the property of the side and should be returned to the side when members leave
  7. The Costumier is responsible for cataloguing, reviewing and suggesting changes to the costume
  8. The Costumier is responsible for monitoring the standard of costume
  9. Upon the loss of an item of costume, the side member is responsible for replacing it at their own cost
  10. The Costumier will provide a guide and advice on how to produce constructed items of costume, e.g. the baldricks and bells
  11. The Costumier will be responsible for maintaining and storing the number and condition of sticks

7. Finance

  1. Spending will be agreed by a majority of the side members after all have been consulted either digitally or in person - refer to the documented Expense Process
  2. Subs will be paid in full by all side members
  3. We will maintain a bank account for all morris related funds which will have at least two signatories
  4. Accounts will be presented at each AGM and a current view of funds will be available on a spreadsheet to all members

8. AGM

  1. The annual general meeting (AGM) will be held once per year.
  2. The notification of the meeting, the draft agenda and venue will be communicated 4 weeks in advance of the meeting either by WhatsApp, email or verbally.
  3. All side members can attend. Only those who have paid the most recent subscription are eligible to vote.
  4. The chair will be agreed at the start of the meeting.
  5. The AGM will only be quorate with 50% of the membership - if this cannot be achieved, the meeting must be adjourned and rearranged at the earliest possible date.
  6. A member present will be appointed to take the minutes.
  7. Only those present and eligible may vote.
  8. Constitutional changes can only be made if received as proposals and published two weeks before the meeting.
  9. Constitutional changes must be agreed with a 75% majority of all eligible to vote whether present at the meeting or not.
  10. Motions may be made and must be seconded, after which they can be carried by a simple majority of those present and eligible to vote.
  11. The chair has the casting vote.

The agenda will take the following form:

  1. Apologies for absence
  2. Minutes to be checked and agreed (circulated in meeting papers)
  3. Matters arising from the minutes
  4. Annual reports from the officers
  5. Subscription review
  6. Kit and costume review
  7. Practice night and venue
  8. Motions for discussion (circulated in meeting papers)
  9. Any other business
  10. Thanks to outgoing officers
  11. Date of the next AGM

The meeting papers will be circulated at least two weeks prior to the meeting

9. EGM

An extraordinary general meeting (EGM) must be called within three weeks of a valid request. A valid request is:

  1. At least 20% of the members or 4 members, whichever is the greater, with a clear proposal(s) for resolution or:
  2. Agreed by the executive officers

Papers for an EGM must be shared at least two weeks in advance of the meeting with clearly stated motions for resolution.

10. Assets of the side

  1. The name of the team
  2. Dance repertoire of any made-up tradition/style
  3. Team records (scrapbooks, dance lists, AGM minutes)
  4. Equipment, e.g. parts of kit; musical instruments; sticks; hankies; drums, first aid kit, catering items, etc. Kit not purchased by individual members remains the property of the team and should be returned when members leave.
  5. The money in the bank account
  6. Digital asset, including documents, photographs and website, together with any copyright or intellectual property therein.

In the event of a break up of the side an agreement will be attempted to be made with respect to the above assets.

In the event of an irreconcilable team split with most members wishing to continue dancing, the assets of the team will belong to the majority group. In the event of both groups being of equal size, the assets will belong to the group with the most elected officers. If this is equal, the assets will belong to the group containing the elected squire.

11. In the event of the team folding

  1. In the following section, "members" are those persons defined in section 2 above and whose subscription is still valid at the time the decision to fold is taken.
  2. The decision to fold must be taken at a General Meeting (GM)
  3. The assets of the side will be disposed of as follows:
    1. The name of the team will be given up
    2. Dance repertoire will be lodged with the Morris Federation
    3. Team records (scrapbooks, dance lists, AGM minutes) will be lodged with the Morris Federation
    4. Kit not purchased by individual members which was the property of the team will be offered first to the members who hold them for purchase for the sum of £1 in full and final settlement. Any equipment (e.g. sticks, spare hankies, drums, stick bags, first aid kit and catering items) will first be offered to members for a reasonable market price.
  4. "Branded" items still in the team's possession after offering to members may, at the discretion of the GM, be destroyed rather than offered to others.
  5. Finally, any items of any sort not thus disposed of will be offered to other Morris team(s) or community groups with similar aims or to the Morris Federation by agreement of a majority of members. The GM may appoint a member or members in committee to arrange such disposal with delegated power to decide on the recipient, and/or timing and/or price (if any). Any items not disposed of will be given to charity.
  6. The money in the bank account, which shall include all receipts from the disposal of equipment shall be donated as follows, in this order:
    1. to charity - there is a tradition that a percentage of our annual collection is given to charity. At least this percentage of the total funds must be donated to a charity, either as agreed at the GM or to the last charity in receipt of team funds before the team folded. The EGM may decide to increase the percentage up to and including 100% but it may not donate less than the traditional percentage.
    2. to members - all members may receive an amount not exceeding their last annual subscription payment. The GM may not increase this limit but has the power to reduce this payment "pro-rata", i.e. by that proportion of the subscription that corresponds to the proportion of the calendar year (subscriptions take effect from Jan 1st) that has already elapsed.
    3. any remaining funds to be donated to the Morris Federation with a request that it be used to preserve and make available the team's dance repertoire, visual record (photographs, scrapbooks etc), and team archive in that order.
  7. Digital assets, including documents, photographs and website, together with any copyright or intellectual property therein shall be archived and the archive offered to the Morris Federation. Team accounts with social media (etc.) shall be closed down.
